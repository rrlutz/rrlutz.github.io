\documentstyle[12pt]{article} \setlength{\topmargin}{-.5in} \setlength{\textheight}{9in} \setlength{\textwidth}{6.5in} \setlength{\oddsidemargin}{0in} \setlength{\evensidemargin}{0in} \newlength{\Num} \setlength{\parindent}{0in} \pagestyle{empty} \begin{document} \begin{center} {\Large\bf Robyn Lutz: Statement of Research} \end{center} My research is in two overlapping areas of software engineering: (1) how to build safe systems and (2) how to specify and analyze requirements. \\ In the first area, my work focuses on software system safety. In particular, I am interested in developing safety analysis techniques for high-integrity product lines. I am also working on architectural analysis techniques for safety-critical systems. My vision for future research in this area is detailed in my recent survey, ``Software Engineering for Safety: A Roadmap,'' available on my web page (http://www.cs.iastate.edu/\symbol {"7E}rlutz)\\ In the second area, my interest is in modeling and analyzing requirements for fault detection and recovery. Many of my results in this area have been applied to NASA spacecraft. Currently, I am also beginning a funded research project to characterize critical software faults that escape testing to manifest themselves during flight. \end{document}
