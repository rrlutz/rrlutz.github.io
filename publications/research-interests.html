Research Interests

My research is in two overlapping areas of software engineering:
    • how to build safe systems, and
    • how to specify and analyze requirements.
In the first area, my work focuses on software system safety. In particular, I am interested in developing safety analysis techniques for high-integrity product lines. I am also working on architectural analysis techniques for safety-critical systems. My vision for future research in this area is detailed in my recent survey, ``Software Engineering for Safety: A Roadmap.'' 

In the second area, my interest is in modeling and analyzing requirements for fault detection and recovery. Many of my results in this area have been applied to NASA spacecraft. Currently, I am also beginning a funded research project to characterize critical software faults that escape testing to manifest themselves during flight.
